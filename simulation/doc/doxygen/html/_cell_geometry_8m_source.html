00004 % @name             Cell geometry
00005 % @description
00006 %
00007 % The Cell Shape process calculates the length and surface area of the cell
00008 % across its lifespan. It also keeps track of the cell volume. The cell is
00009 % approximated to be of a rod shape similar to E. coli, even though
00010 % M. genitalium is known to have a less uniform flask shape. Initially, the
00011 % cell is modeled as a cylinder with two hemispherical caps. Once cell
00012 % pinching commences at the midline of the cell, the shape and size of a
00013 % "septum region" is also modeled.
00014 %
00015 % General geometric equations to represent the shape of a cell, and the
00016 % idea that the density of the cell does not change across the cell cycle
00017 % are borrowed from Domach et al. (1983). We add the assumption that the
00018 % width of the cell remains constant during the lifespan of a cell. Thus,
00019 % the density and cell width are inputs into our model. The cell density of
00020 % E. coli is used 1100g/L (Baldwin et al., 1995). The cell width is
00021 % calculated based on the initial cell mass and density and the assumption
00022 % that the cell is a sphere. The initial cell mass is fit to result in a
00023 % cell width of 200nm (Lind et al, 1984).
00024 %
00025 % Our model calculates the mass of the cell at all timesteps. The mass and
00026 % constant density provide us with the volume at all time points.
00027 % Volume = [2 hemispheres] + [2 cylinders] + [septum region]
00028 % The volume of the septum region is calculated as a cylinder of length,
00029 % 2*septumLength, and width of the cell. Then two cones (height=septum,
00030 % radius=septum) are subtracted from this cylinder.
00031 % (This approximation was used in Shuler et al. 1979).
00032 %
00033 % Since we know the volume of the cell from the cell's mass and density,
00034 % and the septum length from our cytokinesis process, the volume formula
00035 % gives us the length of the cell at each timestep.
00036 %
00037 % Similarly, we can also calculate the surface area of the cell.
00038 % Surface Area = [2 hemispheres] + [2 cylinders] + [septum region]
00039 % The surface area of the septum region is calculated as a cylinder of
00040 % length, 2*septum, and width of the cell.
00041 %

00043 % ==================

00045 %    Growth of a Single Bacteria Cell. Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences

00210             if pinchedDiameter > 0
00211                 %calculate dimensions of cell whose shape is a cyclinder with
00212                 %hemispherical caps
00213                 
00214                 w = width;
00215                 s = (w - pinchedDiameter)/2; %m
00216                 
00217                 cylindricalLength = ...                                             %(m)
