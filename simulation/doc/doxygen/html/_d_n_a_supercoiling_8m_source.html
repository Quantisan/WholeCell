00005 % @description
00006 %   Biology
00007 %   ===========
00008 %   DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV each use 2 ATP to induce 2
00009 %   negative
00010 %   supercoils each time they act. Occasionally, topoisomerase IV can also
00011 %   induce positive supercoils but that is not considered in our model.
00012 %   Topoisomerase I acts to induce positive supercoils. Gyrase, topoisomerase
00013 %   IV, and topoisomerase I act at rates of 1.2, 2.5, and 1 strand passing
00014 %   events per second respectively.
00015 % 
00016 %   We use a calculation of the DNA's linking number (LK) in order to track
00017 %   the supercoiling of the DNA.  The &delta;LK is the difference between the
00018 %   current level of DNA supercoiling and the relaxed level of DNA
00019 %   supercoiling. The LKrelaxed is defined as number of base pairs/10.5, in which
00020 %   10.5 is the number of bases per turn in a relaxed double helix. As the
00021 %   replication loops move, the LKcurrent deviates from this relaxed state,
00022 %   and gyrases and topoisomerases help bring the DNA back to the relaxed
00023 %   state. The superhelical density, or specific linking number density,
00024 %   &sigma;<sub>sp</sub>, is defined as the:
00025 %      (LKcurrent - LKrelaxed)/LKrelaxed. 
00026 %   The activity of gyrases and topoisomerases depends on the &sigma;<sub>sp</sub>
00027 %   of the DNA. Although there is likely a more complex relationship between the
00028 %   DNA supercoiling and enzyme activity, we model the enzyme activity as a
00029 %   combination of step functions and logistic functions. 
00030 %   Topoisomerase IV can only act if &sigma;<sub>sp</sub> is
00031 %   higher than 0. Topoisomerase I can only act if &sigma;<sub>sp</sub> is lower
00032 %   than zero. Gyrase can only act if &sigma;<sub>sp</sub> is higher than -0.1.
00033 %   Within, the regions where gyrase and topoI are allowed to act, we apply
00034 %   a logistic function describing the probability of activity, centered
00035 %   around equilibrium sigma, -0.06. For TopoI, the probability of activity
00036 %   approaches 1 as the sigma gets more positive. For gyrase, the
00037 %   probability of activity approaches 1 as
00038 %   
00039 %   We model up to three regions on the chromosomes and track their LKs
00040 %   separately. Before replication, "unreplicated DNA" is the only region
00041 %   present. The enzymes may act on the chromosome affecting the LKcurrent,
00042 %   and experimentally it has been shown that the enzymes would obtain an
00043 %   LKcurrent such that the steady state &sigma;<sub>sp</sub> is =-0.06. As the
00044 %   replication loop progresses, "unreplicated DNA" is the region downstream of
00045 %   the two replication loops.  The number of bases in this region decreases
00046 %   during replication, meaning that the LKrelaxed decreases. The LKcurrent then,
00047 %   is too high, and must be brought down towards the LKrelaxed by inducing
00048 %   negative supercoils.
00049 %   
00050 %   The second and third regions are the replicated DNA upstream of the
00051 %   replication loops on each of the two chromosomes. As new uncoiled DNA is
00052 %   formed, it is already coiled, but gyrases and topoisomerases can continue
00053 %   to act on this DNA, ideally maintaining the steady state &sigma;<sub>sp</sub>.
00054 %   After replication is complete, these two regions are the only two that exist.
00055 %   
00056 %   It is essential that the &detlta;LK in the region downstream of the replication
00057 %   loops be brought down to 0 by the end of replication.
00058 %   
00059 %   Another consideration is the processivity of the enzymes when bound to the
00060 %   DNA. Topoisomerase IV is highly processive and will stay bound to the DNA
00061 %   as long as the &sigma;<sub>sp</sub> is greater than zero. Topoisomerase I is
00062 %   not highly processive, and essentially acts on the DNA and falls back off
00063 %   right away gyrase will stay bound to the DNA for about 30-60 seconds. We model
00064 %   gyrase processivity as a poisson distribution with &lambda; = 45 seconds.
00065 %   
00066 %   In this process, we go through all free gyrases and topoisomerases in
00067 %   random order,  determine what regions they can bind in, and randomly bind
00068 %   them to a large enough open position on the DNA. We adjust the linking
00069 %   number based on all enzyme actions, and account for the usage of ATP. We
00070 %   also track the processivity of gyrase and topoisomerase IV, and unbind
00071 %   them from the DNA when appropriate.
00072 %   
00073 %   If replication is in progress, we first knock off any enzymes that the
00074 %   replication loop collides into.
00075 %
00076 %   There is an effect of supercoiling on the probabilities of gene 
00077 %   transcription (Peter 2004). While fold change at differnt sigmas have
00078 %   been calculated for many E. coli genes, here, for simplicity, we are
00079 %   only including the effects on the 5 supercoiling genes: gyrB, gyrA,
00080 %   parC, parE, and topA. These genes exist in 3 transcription units. Peter
00081 %   2004 has data for the fold change of expression of each of these genes
00082 %   are various values of sigma (ranging from sigma -0.06-0.02) at various 
00083 %   experimental conditions. Due to the limited data and large variation within
00084 %   the data, we have decided to use a linear fit of the fold change data and 
00085 %   extrapolate the linear fit within the sigma range of -0.08 to 0.07 where 
00086 %   the linear fit seems reasonable. Outside of this range, we estimate a
00087 %   constant fold change of expression. While there is a separate set of
00088 %   data for each of the 5 genes, our model requires a single probability
00089 %   of transcription for each transcription unit. The data for the first
00090 %   gene in each transcription unit is used (gyrB, and parE). TopA is
00091 %   transcribed with genes that are not supercoiling related. The
00092 %   expression of those genes (MG_119, 120, 121) will also get affected by 
00093 %   this process. The general trend is that gyrase and topoIV will have an
00094 %   increased expression when sigma is higher than the equilibrium, and
00095 %   that topoI will have a higher expression when sigma is lower than the
00096 %   equilibrium. Fold changes for gyrase will vary between 0.14 and 6.57.
00097 %   Fold changes for topoIV will vary between 0.98 and 1.14. Fold changes
00098 %   for topoI will vary between 0.042 and 1.15.
