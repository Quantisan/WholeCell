00006 %   Biology
00007 %   ===============
00008 %   To grow and replicate cells must uptake or produce their building blocks and
00009 %   intermediate energy stores, particularly nucleic and amino acids and lipids,
00010 %   as well as secrete metabolites such as modified nucleic acids which it
00011 %   cannot catabolize and extract usable material or energy from. Furthermore,
00012 %   the import/synthesis and export/breakdown of metabolites must be carefully
00013 %   matched to the metabolic demands of the cell to ensure that cellular
00014 %   processes aren't limited by too few metabolites or poisoned by too many
00015 %   metabolites.
00016 %
00017 %   This process simulates

00022 %     maturation within the membrane

00087 %   Simulation
00088 %   ===============
00089 %   Transport and metabolism are modeled using the constraint-based method
00090 %   flux-balance analysis (FBA). Briefly, FBA assumes that bacteria have evolved
00091 %   to maximize growth, and poses transport and metabolism as the optimization
00092 %   of cellular building block and energy production subject to available
00093 %   nutrients, and allowed chemical reactions. FBA models are typically
00094 %   constrained by experimentally measured transport and diffusion rates. In
00095 %   addition to these constraints, we constrain constrain our FBA process by our
00096 %   model's predicted enzyme abundances, and by experimentally measured kinetic
00097 %   parameters. These additional constraints yield a more accurate model of
00098 %   transport and metabolism. Furthermore, we use our other processes to obtain a
00099 %   more extensive and accurate metabolic objective than used in earlier FBA
